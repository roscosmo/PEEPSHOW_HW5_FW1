# Temporal World And NPC Routines

The world should feel alive without waiting for the player.

NPCs travel, leave buildings, gather, disappear, perform hidden activities, and participate in rituals.

## Real-World Time

The world uses real-world time for:

- day/night cycles
- NPC schedules
- environmental events
- rituals
- social behavior
- creature tendencies
- rare encounters

The game expresses time needs through Engine runtime intent. Platform time and sleep policy remain owned by [[Power_and_Sleep_Policy]].

## Routine Design

NPCs should have observable behavior:

- home routines
- work or travel routines
- gathering routines
- suspicious absences
- ritual attendance
- secret movement at unusual hours

## Discovery Style

The player should discover by inference, not by objective markers.

A good routine makes the player ask:

- "Why is that person outside?"
- "Where did they go every third night?"
- "Why does the slime react here?"
- "Why is this place active at midnight?"

## Midnight Events

Midnight ritual events should be rare, atmospheric, and memorable.

They should not become repetitive chores.

See [[Death_and_Resurrection_Ritual]] and [[Observation_and_Temporal_Ecology]].

# Art Audio And Presentation

The Reference Game should embrace the strengths and limits of the HW5 presentation stack.

## Monochrome Strengths

The 1-bit display supports:

- silhouettes
- dithering
- hard contrast
- expressive eyes
- ambiguity
- shadow
- ritual imagery
- strong readable forms

See [[Display_and_Rendering_Contract]].

## Slime Animation Language

The slime should animate through:

- wobble
- slosh
- stretch
- splatter
- inertia
- instability
- imperfect mimicry

Persistent identity comes from eyes, motion, and timing, not high-color detail.

## Large Portrait Art

Large face-on sprites should support:

- realtime combat attacks
- dialogue emphasis
- emotional scenes
- rituals
- transformations
- the feeling of something looking back through the screen

## Audio Direction

Audio should use both major HW5 audio paths:

- speaker music and SFX through [[Audio_Contract]]
- BBB beeps, boops, buzzes, patterns, and ritual tones through the procedural buzzer path

Audio should reinforce:

- creature vocalization
- low-battery care
- ritual timing
- combat punctuation
- unsettling world events

## Asset Pipeline Needs

Art and audio assets should be validated through [[Asset_Pipeline_and_Package_Tooling_Contract]] and packaged through [[Package_Contract]].

Important future constraints:

- tile format
- portrait format
- animation frame budget
- ADPCM audio budget
- BBB pattern representation
- realtime burst asset preloading
